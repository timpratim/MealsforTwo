# Cassava fries from fresh cassava (air fryer method)

What you need (makes ~2–3 portions)

Fresh cassava/yuca: about 1 lb (450 g) peeled weight
(If you have whole unpeeled roots, buy closer to 1.3–1.5 lb / 600–700 g because peel + core get removed.)

Salt: 1 tsp (5 g) for boiling water + more to finish

Neutral oil (sunflower/canola): 1–1½ Tbsp (14–21 g)

Optional crisp booster: cornstarch 1 Tbsp (8 g) or rice flour 1 Tbsp (8 g)

Optional flavor: smoked paprika 1 tsp (2 g), garlic powder 1/2 tsp (1 g)


Step 1) Peel correctly (important for texture)

1. Cut cassava into 3–4 inch (8–10 cm) segments.


2. Slice lengthwise through the thick brown skin and white layer beneath it.


3. Pry the peel off in sheets.



Tip: If you see any gray/blue streaking or it smells “off,” skip it—fresh cassava should be clean, white, and mildly nutty.

Step 2) Remove the woody core

Cassava has a tough fibrous “string” in the center.

Cut into slabs, then batons. As you cut, you’ll see the core—trim it out with a knife (like removing a tough apple core strip).

Aim for fry sticks about 3/8–1/2 inch (1–1.5 cm) thick.


Step 3) Parboil (this sets the fluffy interior)

1. Put cassava in a pot and cover with plenty of water.


2. Add 1 tsp (5 g) salt.


3. Bring to a boil, then simmer 10–15 minutes, until:

the outside looks slightly translucent, and

a knife goes in with some resistance (you don’t want it falling apart).




Science: boiling gelatinizes cassava starch, giving you that creamy interior; the later high heat dries and crisps the surface.

Step 4) Dry thoroughly (crispness lives here)

1. Drain well.


2. Spread on a tray to steam-dry 5 minutes.


3. Then rough them up gently (shake in a colander or toss carefully).
This creates micro-fractures = more crispy edges.



Best extra step (highly recommended): chill or freeze:

Fridge: 20–30 minutes

Freezer: 10–15 minutes
This drives off surface moisture and firms them up so they crisp faster.


Step 5) Season + coat

Toss with:

1–1½ Tbsp (14–21 g) oil

Optional: 1 Tbsp (8 g) cornstarch (big crunch upgrade)

Optional spices (paprika/garlic powder)


Step 6) Air-fry

Preheat air fryer to 200°C / 390°F

Cook in a single layer (work in batches).

Air-fry 12–18 minutes, shaking/turning at 6–8 minutes.

If you want deeper browning, finish 2–3 minutes at 205°C / 400°F.


Finish

Immediately toss with fine salt (or flaky salt) and a squeeze of lime/lemon if serving with spicy sauces.


---

Common mistakes (and fixes)

Not crispy: they weren’t dry enough → steam-dry + chill/freeze before air-frying; don’t overcrowd.

Falling apart: overboiled → stop when just knife-tender, not mushy.

Tough/stringy bites: core wasn’t removed → trim that center strip.

Bland interior: add salt to the boil (it seasons the starch as it hydrates).



---

If you want “next-level” crisp (optional)

Do a quick double-cook:

1. Air-fry at 190°C / 375°F for 10 minutes (set structure)


2. Cool 5 minutes


3. Air-fry at 205°C / 400°F for 6–10 minutes (crisp + color)




---

Plating tip (so it looks restaurant-y)

Pile fries in a tight mound for height, then:

drizzle a little peri-peri around the base (not over everything),

add a small ramekin of chimichurri or aioli,

finish with micro cilantro or flat-leaf parsley leaves + a pinch of flaky salt.
