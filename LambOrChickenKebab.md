# Lamb mince hybrid shawarma / kebab-shop kebab

This lands between lamb shawarma, seekh, and shop-style döner meat. It is deeply savory, slightly red, warmly spiced, and ideal for skewers, patties, or a compact loaf you can slice.

Ingredients

For 2 lb (900 g) lamb mince, ideally 15–20% fat:

lamb mince, 2 lb (900 g)

onion, very finely grated, 1 medium / 5 oz (140 g), then squeeze out excess water

garlic, finely grated, 5 cloves / 0.9 oz (25 g)

tomato paste, 1 tbsp (16 g)

Turkish red pepper paste or mild chili paste, 1 tbsp (16 g)

plain full-fat yogurt, 2 tbsp (30 g)

olive oil, 1 tbsp (14 g)

kosher salt, 2 tsp (12 g)

black pepper, 1 tsp (2 g)

ground cumin, 2 tsp (4 g)

ground coriander, 2 tsp (4 g)

sweet paprika, 2 tsp (4 g)

smoked paprika, 1/2 tsp (1 g)

Aleppo pepper or chili flakes, 1 tsp (2 g), adjust to heat preference

ground allspice, 3/4 tsp (1.5 g)

ground cinnamon, 1/4 tsp (0.5 g)

dried oregano, 1/2 tsp (0.5 g)

dried mint, 1/2 tsp (0.5 g)

fresh parsley, very finely chopped, 1/4 cup (10 g)

baking soda, 1/4 tsp (1 g), optional but useful for a looser, juicier kebab-shop texture


Why this works

Cumin + coriander give the backbone most people associate with both shawarma and red shop kebab.

Allspice + a little cinnamon create the sweet-warm aromatic lift common in Levantine lamb blends.

Tomato paste + pepper paste add sweetness, glutamates, and a red roasted note.

Mint + oregano are kept very low so they brighten without turning the mixture “herby.”

Baking soda raises surface pH slightly, which can improve browning and tenderness if used sparingly.


Method

1. Prep the onion properly.
Grate the onion very finely, then squeeze it hard in a towel or your hands. You want the flavor, not all the water. Excess onion water makes the kebab loose and spongy.


2. Mix the seasoning base.
In a bowl, combine onion, garlic, tomato paste, pepper paste, yogurt, oil, salt, and all spices.


3. Work the mince.
Add the lamb and mix thoroughly for 2 to 3 minutes until the meat becomes tacky and cohesive. This extraction of myosin helps the kebab hold together and slice cleanly later.


4. Rest the mixture.
Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, ideally 4 to 12 hours. This allows the salt to fully distribute and the spice profile to bloom.


5. Shape.
You have 3 good options:

Skewers: form long cylinders around flat skewers

Patties: shape into oval kebabs

Loaf: press tightly into a parchment-lined loaf pan for slicing later



6. Cook.

Grill / broiler: High heat, about 4 to 6 minutes per side, until well browned and cooked through

Loaf method: Bake at 425°F (220°C) for 20 to 30 minutes, then broil briefly for color

Target an internal temperature of at least 160°F (71°C) for ground lamb



7. Rest before slicing.
Rest 5 to 10 minutes so juices redistribute and the protein network firms slightly.



Best results

Lamb mince should not be too lean. Fat is critical for that rich shaved-kebab impression.

Do not overdo yogurt; too much softens the mixture and weakens browning.

If the mixture feels loose, add 1 to 2 tbsp (8 to 16 g) fine breadcrumbs, but only if needed.

2) Chicken mince hybrid shawarma / kebab-shop kebab

This version is more aggressively seasoned because chicken is milder and leaner. It has the red takeaway-shop flavor profile many people expect.

Ingredients

For 2 lb (900 g) chicken mince, ideally a mix of thigh and breast or all thigh:

chicken mince, 2 lb (900 g)

onion, very finely grated, 1 medium / 5 oz (140 g), squeezed dry

garlic, finely grated, 6 cloves / 1 oz (30 g)

ginger, finely grated, 1 tbsp (15 g)

tomato paste, 1 1/2 tbsp (24 g)

Turkish red pepper paste or mild chili paste, 1 tbsp (16 g)

plain full-fat yogurt, 3 tbsp (45 g)

olive oil or neutral oil, 1 tbsp (14 g)

kosher salt, 2 1/4 tsp (14 g)

black pepper, 1 tsp (2 g)

ground cumin, 2 tsp (4 g)

ground coriander, 2 tsp (4 g)

sweet paprika, 2 tsp (4 g)

smoked paprika, 1 tsp (2 g)

turmeric, 1/2 tsp (1 g)

Aleppo pepper or chili flakes, 1 tsp (2 g)

ground allspice, 1/2 tsp (1 g)

dried oregano, 1/2 tsp (0.5 g)

dried mint, 1/4 tsp (0.25 g)

fresh parsley, finely chopped, 1/4 cup (10 g)

baking soda, 1/4 tsp (1 g), optional

fine breadcrumbs or panko, 1/4 cup (25 g), optional if your mince is very wet


Why this works

Chicken benefits from ginger + garlic more than lamb does; the aromatics help compensate for lower intrinsic fat flavor.

Turmeric adds background earthiness and the warm golden-red tone associated with many shawarma-style chicken mixes.

Slightly more paprika and paste gives that familiar takeaway color and sweetness.

Using some thigh meat improves flavor because thigh contains more fat and myoglobin than breast.


Method

1. Remove excess moisture.
Squeeze the grated onion well. Chicken mince is especially prone to going soft and wet.


2. Build the paste.
Combine onion, garlic, ginger, tomato paste, pepper paste, yogurt, oil, salt, and spices until smooth.


3. Mix thoroughly.
Add the chicken mince and work it for 2 to 3 minutes until sticky and evenly seasoned. If it feels sloppy, add the breadcrumbs and mix again.


4. Rest.
Cover and refrigerate for 2 to 12 hours. Chicken especially benefits from this because salt and aromatics need time to penetrate and bind.


5. Shape.
Best formats:

compact skewers

a pressed sheet-pan slab for slicing into strips

a loaf for shawarma-style slices



6. Cook.

Grill / broiler: Cook over high heat, turning carefully, until nicely charred

Oven loaf or slab: Bake at 425°F (220°C) until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C), then broil for extra color

Ground chicken must be fully cooked



7. Rest and slice.
Rest 5 minutes, then slice thinly or chop for wraps.



Best results

All-thigh chicken mince gives the most shop-like result.

Too much yogurt or wet onion will make the texture pasty.

Chicken likes a little more salt and spice than lamb because it is a quieter canvas.



---

Flavor adjustment guide

For a more shawarma-leaning profile

Increase:

allspice slightly

cinnamon very slightly

coriander

garlic


Add:

1 tsp (2 g) ground cardamom for the chicken version only if you want a more Levantine aromatic profile


For a more kebab-shop red profile

Increase:

tomato paste

pepper paste

paprika

chili flakes


Reduce:

cinnamon

mint


For a more “döner loaf” sliceable texture

Mix the meat longer until sticky

Press very tightly into a loaf pan

Chill thoroughly before baking

After baking, chill again, then slice and re-crisp in a hot pan


That two-stage cook is very effective because it creates more browned edges, and that browned crust is where much of the “kebab shop” flavor lives.


---

Common mistakes

Using onion purée without squeezing it: too wet, weak browning

Too much yogurt: soft texture, less crust

Too little fat: dry, crumbly kebab

Under-mixing: poor bind, fragile skewers

Too many herbs: can make it taste like meatballs rather than kebab



---

Serving and finishing

For the most convincing shop-style result, finish the cooked meat with:

a light sprinkle of sumac

a tiny pinch of flaky salt

a brush of hot fat or melted butter with paprika

a squeeze of lemon only at serving, not in the raw mix


For plating or wraps, pair with:

pickled red onion

shredded lettuce or cabbage

tomato

toasted pita or flatbread

garlic sauce, tahini sauce, or chili sauce depending on whether you want it more Turkish, Levantine, or takeaway-shop
