# Pressure Cooker Chicken Soup

Ingredients

600 g chicken bones, with any attached meat

250 g soup vegetable mix with onion, carrot, celery, and leek

2 garlic cloves, lightly crushed

1 teaspoon tomato paste (5 g)

1 small Parmesan rind, about 10 to 15 g

1 small piece kombu, about 3 to 5 g

4 to 6 parsley stems

2 to 3 small dill stems

1 bay leaf

6 to 8 black peppercorns

3 3/4 to 4 cups water (900 mL to 1 L)

1 teaspoon salt (5 g) to start


To finish

1 to 2 teaspoons miso (6 to 12 g)

50 to 100 g enoki mushrooms, trimmed and separated

2.8 to 3.5 oz thin rice noodles (80 to 100 g)

1 tablespoon chopped parsley leaves (4 g)

1 to 2 teaspoons chopped dill (1 to 2 g)

Black pepper, to taste

Lemon juice, a few drops only if needed


Method

1. Build the base

Use sauté mode.

Add a little oil if needed. Add the tomato paste (5 g) and cook for about 30 seconds to remove the raw taste.

Add the vegetable mix and garlic. Cook 2 to 3 minutes, just until the vegetables begin to soften. Do not brown deeply.

2. Add the soup ingredients

Add:

chicken bones

Parmesan rind

parsley stems

dill stems

bay leaf

peppercorns

water (900 mL to 1 L)

salt (5 g)


If you have not added the kombu yet, keep it out for now and steep it later.
If you already added it, that is fine.

3. Pressure cook

Cook on high pressure for 30 minutes.

Let the pressure release naturally.

4. Remove only the big items

Since you do not want to strain the soup, just remove:

bones

Parmesan rind

bay leaf

kombu, if it is still in there

any large herb stems you can easily grab


Pick off any meat from the bones and return it to the pot.

Leave the vegetables in the soup.

5. Add kombu only as a steep if not already used

If the kombu was not pressure-cooked, add it now to the hot soup and let it steep for 8 to 10 minutes, then remove.

This gives better umami with less risk of a muddy seaweed note.

6. Add enoki

Bring the soup to a gentle simmer and add the enoki. Cook 1 to 2 minutes.

7. Add the miso properly

Turn the heat to low or off.

Put the miso (6 to 12 g) in a small bowl, add a few spoonfuls of hot broth, and stir until smooth. Then stir that mixture back into the soup.

Do not boil after adding miso. Boiling dulls its aroma and makes the flavor harsher.

8. Noodles

Cook the thin rice noodles (80 to 100 g) separately according to the package. Drain and place in bowls.

Ladle the soup over the noodles.

9. Finish

Top with chopped parsley and a little chopped dill. Add black pepper to taste.

Only add a few drops of lemon juice if the soup tastes heavy. With miso, Parmesan rind, and tomato paste already in play, keep lemon very light.

Final balance

This soup should taste:

chicken-forward

savory and rounded

lightly herbal

not obviously cheesy

not obviously seaweed-like

not obviously tomatoey

gently enriched by the miso


Important note

Because you added Parmesan rind + kombu + tomato paste + miso, the broth has a lot of umami boosters. That can be excellent, but keep the finishing restrained:

go light on extra salt

go light on lemon

keep dill modest


Taste first, then adjust.

Best rescue if it tastes too intense

Add a little hot water, about 60 to 120 mL, then taste again. That usually fixes an over-concentrated broth better than adding more acid.

Serving

Put noodles in the bowl first, then the soup, then the fresh herbs on top. That keeps the herbs bright and gives a cleaner, more elegant finish.
